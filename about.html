Sarah Yasuda is a library clerk at the Boston Architectural College and a MLIS candidate at Simmons College School of Library and Information Science (concentration in Cultural Heritage Informatics). With her extensive experience working in the education field and abroad, her passion is rooted in equitable access to education and service to the community.

During her undergraduate studies at UCLA, she received the U.S. Department of State's Benjamin A. Gilman Scholarship to travel to Pune, India where she conducted research on multilingual communities in the city. Graduating with a B.A. in Anthropology and a Minor in Global Studies, she was then accepted into the JET Program to work as an assistant teacher and translator at a private school in Tokyo, Japan. There she worked as a translator, an interpreter and liason to overseas organizations, and an instructor. During her time in Japan, she also did freelance work as a translator and interpreter.

Now based in Boston, she is pursuing a career in the library and information science field. She currently works in acquisitions and catalogues at the Boston Architectural College. Passionate about equitable access to information and technology, she hopes to work as a systems librarian or in digital preservation in the near future.
