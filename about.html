I am currently a library clerk at the Boston Architectural College and a MLIS candidate at Simmons College School of Library and Information Science (concentration in Cultural Heritage Informatics). With my experience working in the education field and abroad, I am passionate about equitable access to education and service to my community.

During my undergraduate studies at UCLA, I received the U.S. Department of State's Benjamin A. Gilman Scholarship to travel to Pune, India where I conducted research on multilingual communities in the city. Graduating with a B.A. in Anthropology and a Minor in Global Studies, I was then accepted into the JET Program to work as an assistant teacher and translator at a private school in Tokyo, Japan. There I worked as a translator, an interpreter and liason to overseas organizations, and an instructor. During my time in Japan, I also did freelance work as a translator and interpreter.

Now based in Boston, I am pursuing a career in the library and information science field. I currently works in acquisitions and catalogues at the Boston Architectural College. Passionate about equitable access to information and technology, I hope to work as a systems librarian and/or in digital preservation in the near future.
